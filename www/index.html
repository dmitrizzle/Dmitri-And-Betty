Tropical mountains of Northern Thailand

Chiang Dao "The Nest 1"

We shall be wed at a small resort, about an hour ride from the city of Chiang Mai. It is situated on the base of a wall of Thai tropical mountains, not far away from their famous caves and a beautiful temple. We chose October since it is at the very end of a rain season when the vegetation is exceptionally lush, the air is cool and clear after a warm rain and, if we're lucky, we'll see some low-hanging clouds grazing the tops just a few hundred meters from us. And don't worry, every during the midst of a rain season, it usually never pours for longer than three hours.

Getting There

We will be providing transportation from the city to the resort for those who need it. There are fourteen huts on premise with your own washroom, although there is no A/C (it shouldn't be too hot during this time, it is in the mountains). Depending on the number of guests accommodations for the night will be paid for by us. But we'll have to give priority to our guests from overseas. If there is no room for you - no worries; there are lots of places within walking distance you can rent.

If you choose to drive there on your own, you can navigate using Google Maps with this link. Keep in mind that although car and motorbike rentals are cheap here (a decent scooter shouldn't cost you more than 200 Baht/day), the cars drive on the left-hand side (think England, Japan). Do give yourself an ample amount of time to learn this and remember that you will be going up mountain roads that aren't maintained as well as they are in Canada. Also, please be very careful and mindful of bad drivers. Thailand is #2 in the world for deaths on the roads. Although there are always lots of foreigners driving here and we never had any problems here. Just be very careful. A bonus: bring an international drivers' license.
